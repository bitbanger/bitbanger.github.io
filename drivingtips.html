Driving tips

A lot of you probably recall that I just got my driver's license recently. Since I've been on the road, I've picked up a few tips that you might like to refresh yourself on:

1. Most US states mandate that you give at least three feet of distance to cyclists, but the axes aren't specified; a sweet jump that clears their helmet by three feet is perfectly acceptable.

2. Drivers of large trucks often have "bald spots" on the backs, sides, and fronts of their heads. For your own safety, try to refrain from getting too close to them.

3. If you come up to a traffic light that's been red for some time, consider it a "stale red" and floor the accelerator as a precaution.

4. If you have the right of way but are still hit by someone who's disobeying the law, the paramedics legally have to try extra hard to save you.

5. You're generally allowed to turn left on a red light from a one way street onto another one way street, however, this is probably an indication that you're lost.
